# Hakivo User Research Study

> **Document Type:** Research Plan
> **Status:** Draft
> **Author:** Tarik Moody
> **Created:** December 2024

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a comprehensive user research study to validate Hakivo's value proposition across multiple audience segments. The study aims to understand willingness to pay, feature preferences, pain points, and unmet needs in the civic engagement space.

---

## 1. Study Objectives

### Primary Objectives

| Objective | Key Question | Success Metric |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Willingness to Pay** | Would users pay $12/month for Hakivo Pro? | >40% of target segments express intent to pay |
| **Feature Validation** | Which features drive the most value? | Ranked feature priority list by segment |
| **Pain Point Discovery** | What frustrates users about staying civically informed? | Categorized pain points with frequency data |
| **Gap Analysis** | What's missing from Hakivo? | Prioritized feature request backlog |
| **Competitive Positioning** | How does Hakivo compare to alternatives? | Competitive matrix with differentiation points |

### Secondary Objectives

- Identify early adopter segments most likely to convert
- Understand current civic engagement habits and tools
- Validate messaging and value proposition language
- Discover potential partnership or distribution channels
- Gather testimonials and case studies for marketing

## 2. Target Audience Segments

### Segment A: Busy Professionals (Primary Target)

**Profile:**
- Age 25-55
- Full-time employed (knowledge workers, managers, executives)
- College-educated
- Votes regularly but feels uninformed about legislation
- Has 15-30 minutes/day for news consumption

**Key Questions:**
- How do you currently stay informed about Congress?
- What would make you feel more civically prepared?
- Would you listen to a 5-minute daily briefing during your commute?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- LinkedIn ads targeting professionals
- Professional associations (Rotary, Chamber of Commerce)
- Coworking spaces and corporate wellness programs

---

### Segment B: Political Activists & Advocates

**Profile:**
- Age 18-65
- Actively involved in causes (environment, healthcare, civil rights, etc.)
- Already tracks some legislation manually
- Member of advocacy organizations
- Donates to political causes

**Key Questions:**
- How do you currently track bills related to your cause?
- What tools do you use? What's frustrating about them?
- Would you pay for a tool that alerts you when relevant bills move?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- Advocacy organizations (Sierra Club, ACLU, NRA, etc.)
- Political subreddits and forums
- Nonprofit mailing lists

---

### Segment C: Journalists & Researchers

**Profile:**
- Political reporters, policy analysts, academic researchers
- Needs accurate, timely legislative data
- Uses multiple sources (Congress.gov, CQ, Bloomberg Government)
- Time-sensitive deadlines

**Key Questions:**
- What's your current workflow for tracking legislation?
- How much time do you spend on legislative research weekly?
- Would AI-generated summaries help or hurt your work?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- Journalism associations (SPJ, ONA)
- Academic institutions (political science departments)
- Press clubs and media organizations

---

### Segment D: Educators (Civics Teachers & Professors)

**Profile:**
- High school civics teachers, college professors
- Needs current examples for curriculum
- Values accuracy and non-partisanship
- Budget-conscious (may need institutional purchase)

**Key Questions:**
- How do you bring current legislation into your classroom?
- Would you use Hakivo as a teaching tool?
- What would make it classroom-ready?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- National Council for the Social Studies
- State teacher associations
- EdTech conferences and forums

---

### Segment E: Nonprofit Organizations

**Profile:**
- Policy directors, government affairs staff
- Small to mid-size nonprofits (limited lobbying budget)
- Tracks legislation relevant to their mission
- Needs to communicate policy updates to members

**Key Questions:**
- How does your organization currently monitor legislation?
- What's your budget for policy tracking tools?
- Would you integrate Hakivo with your member communications?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- Independent Sector, Council on Foundations
- State nonprofit associations
- GuideStar/Candid networks

---

### Segment F: Local Government Officials

**Profile:**
- City council members, county commissioners, mayors
- Part-time elected officials (many have day jobs)
- Needs to understand federal/state impact on local policy
- Resource-constrained

**Key Questions:**
- How do you track federal legislation that affects your community?
- Would you share Hakivo with your constituents?
- What would make you recommend it to other officials?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- National League of Cities
- U.S. Conference of Mayors
- State municipal leagues

---

### Segment G: General Public (Casual Civic Interest)

**Profile:**
- Age 18-70
- Votes sometimes, wants to be more informed
- Gets news from social media and mainstream sources
- Low current engagement with legislation
- Price-sensitive

**Key Questions:**
- What stops you from following Congress more closely?
- If legislation tracking was as easy as checking the weather, would you?
- What would make you upgrade from free to paid?

**Recruitment Channels:**
- Social media advertising (Facebook, Instagram)
- Survey platforms (Prolific, UserTesting)
- Community organizations

## 3. Research Methodology

### Phase 1: Quantitative Survey (Week 1-2)

**Sample Size:** 500-1000 respondents across all segments

**Survey Structure:**
1. **Screening Questions** (segment qualification)
2. **Current Behavior** (how do you stay informed today?)
3. **Pain Points** (what's frustrating about current solutions?)
4. **Feature Prioritization** (rank these 10 features)
5. **Willingness to Pay** (Van Westendorp pricing analysis)
6. **Demographics** (age, education, political engagement level)

**Key Metrics:**
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) for concept
- Feature importance ratings (1-5 scale)
- Price sensitivity curves
- Segment-specific conversion likelihood

---

### Phase 2: Qualitative Interviews (Week 2-3)

**Sample Size:** 5-8 interviews per segment (35-56 total)

**Interview Format:**
- 45-60 minutes via Zoom
- Semi-structured with open-ended questions
- Screen sharing for live product walkthrough
- Recorded with participant consent

**Interview Guide Topics:**
1. **Current State** (walk me through how you stay informed)
2. **Pain Points** (what's the most frustrating part?)
3. **Product Demo** (let me show you Hakivo - think aloud)
4. **Feature Reactions** (what stands out? what's missing?)
5. **Pricing Discussion** (would you pay? how much?)
6. **Closing** (any final thoughts? who else should I talk to?)

---

### Phase 3: Usability Testing (Week 3-4)

**Sample Size:** 5-8 participants per segment

**Tasks to Test:**
1. Find a bill about healthcare and understand what it does
2. Track a bill and explain when you'd be notified
3. Find your representative and their voting record
4. Listen to a daily briefing and summarize what you learned
5. Use the AI assistant to ask a question about legislation

**Metrics:**
- Task completion rate
- Time on task
- System Usability Scale (SUS) score
- Qualitative feedback on friction points

---

### Phase 4: Focus Groups (Week 4)

**Format:** 90-minute virtual focus groups

**Groups:**
- Group 1: Busy Professionals (6-8 participants)
- Group 2: Activists & Advocates (6-8 participants)
- Group 3: Educators & Nonprofits (6-8 participants)

**Discussion Topics:**
- Feature prioritization (dot voting exercise)
- Messaging and positioning (which tagline resonates?)
- Pricing tiers and packaging (what belongs in free vs. pro?)
- Missing features brainstorm (what would make this indispensable?)

## 10. Interview Discussion Guide

### Opening (5 min)
- Thank you for joining. This is research for a civic engagement platform.
- No right or wrong answers - we want your honest reactions.
- We'll record this session - is that okay?

### Current State (10 min)
- Walk me through how you typically stay informed about politics and legislation.
- What tools or sources do you use?
- How much time do you spend on this weekly?
- What's working well? What's frustrating?

### Product Demo (15 min)
- Let me share my screen and show you Hakivo.
- [Show dashboard, bill tracking, audio briefings, AI assistant]
- Think aloud - tell me what you're noticing.
- What stands out as interesting or useful?
- What's confusing or unclear?

### Feature Deep Dive (10 min)
- Which features would you use most often?
- Which features seem least useful to you?
- What's missing that would make this more valuable?
- How does this compare to what you're using now?

### Pricing Discussion (5 min)
- The Pro version is $12/month. What's your reaction?
- At what price would this be a no-brainer for you?
- At what price would it be too expensive?
- What would justify the price for you?

### Closing (5 min)
- Any final thoughts or suggestions?
- Who else do you know who might benefit from this?
- Can I follow up if we have more questions?

## 11. Analysis Framework

### Quantitative Analysis

| Data Type | Analysis Method |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Feature rankings | Mean scores + segment comparison |
| Pricing data | Van Westendorp price sensitivity meter |
| NPS scores | Segment breakdown + driver analysis |
| Demographics | Cross-tabulation with key metrics |
| Completion rates | Task success analysis |

### Qualitative Analysis

| Data Type | Analysis Method |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Interview transcripts | Thematic coding (affinity mapping) |
| Usability observations | Issue severity rating |
| Open-ended responses | Sentiment + keyword clustering |
| Focus group notes | Priority voting results |

### Synthesis Deliverables

1. **Segment Personas** - Updated personas with research validation
2. **Feature Priority Matrix** - Importance vs. satisfaction grid
3. **Competitive Positioning Map** - Where Hakivo wins/loses
4. **Pricing Recommendation** - Optimal price point with justification
5. **Roadmap Inputs** - Prioritized feature requests
6. **Messaging Recommendations** - Language that resonates

## 12. Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Low survey response rate | Multiple recruitment channels, compelling incentive |
| Biased sample (only enthusiasts) | Include disqualified segment in analysis |
| Participants unfamiliar with legislation | Include novice-friendly onboarding in demo |
| Socially desirable responses | Anonymous survey, indirect questioning |
| COVID/scheduling challenges | Fully virtual methodology |

## 13. Ethical Considerations

- **Informed Consent:** All participants will be informed about the study purpose
- **Data Privacy:** No personally identifiable information shared externally
- **Political Neutrality:** Questions and demos avoid partisan framing
- **Incentive Fairness:** Consistent compensation across segments
- **Accessibility:** Accommodations for participants with disabilities
