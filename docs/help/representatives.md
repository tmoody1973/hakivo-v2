# Understanding Your Representatives

Hakivo helps you stay connected with your elected representatives at the federal level. Know who represents you, how they vote, and how to contact them.

## Finding Your Representatives

### Automatic Detection
When you set your ZIP code in Settings, Hakivo automatically identifies:
- Your 2 U.S. Senators
- Your U.S. House Representative

These appear on your dashboard for quick access.

### The Representatives Page
Navigate to **Representatives** for a complete view including:
- Your personal representatives (based on ZIP code)
- Search all members of Congress
- Filter by state or party

## Representative Profiles

Click any representative to see their full profile:

### Basic Information
- Official photo
- Full name and party affiliation
- State and district
- Years in office
- Leadership positions (if any)

### Contact Information
- Washington D.C. office address
- District office locations
- Phone numbers
- Official website
- Social media links

### Committee Assignments
See which committees your representative serves on:
- Committee name and role (Chair, Ranking Member, Member)
- Subcommittee assignments
- Committee jurisdiction

### Voting Record
Review how your representative has voted on key legislation:
- Recent votes with bill titles
- Vote position (Yea, Nay, Present, Not Voting)
- Comparison with party position

### Sponsored Legislation
Bills introduced or co-sponsored by this representative:
- Bill title and number
- Current status
- Number of co-sponsors

## Following Representatives

### How to Follow
Click the **Follow** button on any representative's profile to receive updates.

### What Following Provides
- Notifications when they vote on tracked bills
- Alerts for new sponsored legislation
- Committee hearing participation
- Press releases and statements (when available)

### Managing Followed Representatives
View and manage followed representatives in **Settings > Tracked Items**.

## Searching Representatives

### Search by Name
Type any representative's name in the search box:
- First name, last name, or full name
- Handles common variations

### Filter by State
Use the state dropdown to see all representatives from a specific state.

### Filter by Party
Select Democrat, Republican, or Independent to narrow results.

## Voting Records Explained

### Vote Types
| Vote | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Yea | Voted in favor |
| Nay | Voted against |
| Present | Attended but didn't vote yes or no |
| Not Voting | Did not participate in the vote |

### Vote Context
Each vote shows:
- The bill being voted on
- Brief description of what the vote decided
- How the overall vote turned out
- Whether it passed or failed

### Party Line Votes
We indicate when a representative voted:
- With their party majority
- Against their party majority
- On bipartisan legislation

## Contacting Your Representatives

### Why Contact Them
Representatives track constituent communications. Your voice matters on:
- Bills being considered
- Issues affecting your community
- Requesting assistance with federal agencies

### How to Contact
From any representative profile:
1. Click the phone icon to see their phone number
2. Click the website icon to visit their official site
3. Use the contact form on their website
4. Visit district offices for in-person meetings

### Tips for Effective Contact
1. **Be Specific**: Reference specific bills by number
2. **Be Concise**: State your position clearly
3. **Be Respectful**: Regardless of party differences
4. **Be a Constituent**: Mention you live in their district
5. **Follow Up**: If you don't get a response, try again

## Understanding Congress Structure

### The Senate
- 100 members (2 per state)
- 6-year terms
- Confirms presidential appointments
- Approves treaties

### The House
- 435 members (based on state population)
- 2-year terms
- Initiates revenue bills
- Brings impeachment charges

### Your Representation
You have 3 federal representatives:
- 2 Senators representing your entire state
- 1 House member representing your district

## Troubleshooting

### Wrong Representatives Showing
- Verify your ZIP code in Settings
- Some ZIP codes cross district lines; try your full address
- Contact support if issues persist

### Representative Not Found
- Try different name spellings
- Search by state instead of name
- New members may take a few days to appear

### Outdated Information
- Congressional data updates daily
- Major changes (new members, redistricting) may take longer
